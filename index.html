Open User

About

This was the final project of the Open and User Innovation course (ME1033), at KTH. We were asked to write an essay about any topic of our preference, related to Open and User Innovation. I am very interested in Entrepeneurship for Social Impact, therefore, I chose to write about how Open Innovation can be used for a improving the lives of those in need. The essay I wrote starts witha brief secondary research about Open Innovation, then it explores the different of Social Innovation strategies. I conclude with a presentation of the OpenIDEO project and the reasons that allowed it to be a case of success. My goal with this essay was to highlight the need for more solutions for current social issuesand and to invite the reader to reflect about it.

Abstract

This paper describes examples of Social Innovation projects currently making positive impact in society by giving individuals the support and tools needed for them to create solutions by themselves. Such projects might vary in strategy and focus, however, they were all built to help individuals on the tasks of finding information, learning technical skills and getting in contact with partners. By empowering the users, it is possible to achieve a much greater and broader impact than by relying on single leaders or big corporations. We believe this paper can add much value to the community, by showing how it is possible to combine Open and User Innovation with Social Impact.

Keywords

  • Open and User Innovation
  • Social Impact
  • OpenIDEO

Drive Care

Context

With this project we were one of the winners of the Green Hackathon Smart Cities, Stockholm (12-13th Feb 2016). The challenge was to find a solution for the problem of elderly care in Sweden. Currently, the care takers need to drive themselves to the different houses where they work. We had two days and a group of five members to pitch a feasible and sustainable way to improve the elderly care.

Process

We had a short time to work on an idea that could prove its value to the judges and to the society. We realized that in our case, the best way to solve this problem would be to focus on simplicity and be straigh forward. It was only after an extensive analysis of the current problems care takers face, that we started propsing solutions and grouping them. Our brainsstroming session lasted the whole evening but in the end we were convinced that we had a good point to start with. The next day we focused on creating a excellent picth, designing user interface and researching market trends.

Outcome

We needed to reduce the mileage care takers drove by car every day, without affecting the quality of care for the elderly. We were inspired by Uber and Lyft when we came up with the concept of Drive Care. Caretakers could share rides, with a professional driver that would ride caregivers to destination and pick them up to next place. Route optimization algorithm would decide the best routes, preserving quality indicators and following the care takers schedule. The sustainbale benefits are less pollution and time spent on traffic jams. This solution won first prze in our category and we were invited to implement it at Scania office in Stockholm.

SharedBall

About

This paper is a project I did for the Bachelor Thesis I wrote when compelting my degree in Computer Science. The topic tested Attribution of Responsibility and Sense of Agency using a game I designed and implemented using Unity. This paper was published and presented at the SBGames Brazil 2014.

Abstract

Attribution of responsibility and sense of agency are key topics raised by situations in which control is shared between man and machine. For example, when a failure occurs in a robotic surgery, causing permanent injuries to a patient, who should be blamed? In order to investigate how individuals attribute responsibility and how they perceive agency, a game was used as a playful research tool. The assumption is that a game may lead the player to behave more naturally, forgetting he is in a conducted experiment. This paper presents the Shared Ball game, designed to simulate a situation of shared control. It was hypothesised that users would display a self servicing bias behaviour when playing the game. After conducting an experiment, the hypothesis showed to be consistent. Knowledge acquired in this experiment can be beneficial to the construction of safer and more enjoyable technology, since that all possible abnormal conditions can never be fully predicted. The use of adaptive and autonomous technology has to be accompanied by a debate on moral responsibility and ethical rules.

Main topics

  • Attribution of Responsibility
  • Sense of Agency
  • Unity games as a research method

Influence of color on information retrieval and self-disclosur

About

This was the final report for the Research Methodology course (II2202) at KTH. We were asked to use research methods to investigate a research question, related to Human Computer Interaction. Me and my partner were interested in discovering how they can influence the user experience. We were allowed to use made up data, as long as we had a complete report.

Abstract

This project investigated how colours can influence the user experience whenever they are navigating on a website, specially for understanding information and answering personal questions. An online experiment was conducted with 72 subjects in order to measure time spent to understand the main idea of a paragraph and the number of words written for a personal question. The results showed that the majority of subjects were influenced in the same way by the same colour (which means that the differences between background colours were significant). Therefore, we interpreted these results as an indication that the background colour can play a relevant role in fostering the user experience.

  • Influence of Colours
  • Usability and web design
  • Self-disclore factors

The Tavern

Context

This project was part of the coursework for the Methodology of Interaction Design (IC2005) course at KTH. My group consisted of me and two other classmates. Our task was to follow the brief to the CHI 2016 on Assistive Technology: “design interventions to serve users that present different and often challenging needs”. After four weeks of work, we came up with a social game to be played by stroke patients in recover either in the hospital or at home.

Abstract

The motor and speech impairments of many stroke patients makes them excluded of the possibilities of social contact and entertainment provided by technology. We designed ”The Tavern” as screen interface controlled by speech or gesture, where patients can talk with each other and play games together, the way it was in taverns and traditional bars. Our goal was to help them by giving new interactive ways to enjoy positive social experiences during their recovery.

Process

Our design process was divided in four phases, one phase per week: brainstorming, building personas and scenarios, creating sketches and prototypes, and finally evaluating a prototype.

As we were inspired by the movie “The Diving Bell and the Butterfly”, we wanted to work with recovering stroke patients. We had some brainstorming sessions, using the the traditional method, the Six Thinking Hats method and the bodystorming in the hospital. Then, we collected data from second research, surveys on online communities, semi-structured interviews with experts and relatives.

Using the data collected we built two personas, Frank and Hiltrude, being Hiltrude the secondary one. Then, besides the persona, we also developed a scenario [5] to illustrate a situation when Frank would use our device. Based on the data analysis, personas and scenarios of the previous phase we developed sketches, and later on an interactive prototype to be used for evaluation.

We aimed at conducting field tests with the patients. However, due to time constraints as well as the complexity of reaching them in the hospitals, we performed our evaluations with three other representative users: an experienced stroke nurse and two stroke patients from Germany

Outcome

For the CHI 2016 Design Competition we had to deliver a paper, a poster and a video. In the paper we were asked to describe the proper and refer to previous works. The poster and the video were illustrations of the product we developed in this four weeks.

This project showed me it is extremely challenging to reach patients in the spam of four weeks. I did not have contacts to stroke patients and performing remote evaluations is not ideal. My first lesson was that I need to have enough time to go through the process of contacting patients. Second, it is very important to keep in mind their condition and if they are too vulnerable, it is not ideal to disturb them with interviews.

Gymification

Context

This project was part of the coursework for the Human-computer Interaction: Principles and Design (IC1007) course at KTH. I was working in a group with three other students and we were given the task of researching the needs of personal trainers, then designing an interactive, screen-based prototype. The final products were a PowerPoint prototype and a report describing the user tests we conducted.

Process

In order to identify the needs of personal trainers, we did some secondary research and semi-structured interviews with two employees at the university gym (KTH Halen). We identified two main issues they were facing at that moment: to keep track of the progress of each client and to advertise their services to a broader audience.

Based on these findings we split the team in two group, then we brainstormed and sketched a solution for these problems. We made a paper prototype (low fidelity) to communicate our ideas in a clearer way to the rest of the team. After presenting our ideas, we decided to focus on a gamification solution for recording the improvements of the personal trainer's clients.

Then, we built a high fidelity prototype using PowerPoint, as the brief given to our course required the use of this platform. We took this prototype to the field and we tested it using the Think Aloud method, in three different gyms around Stockholm. We found that many aspects of the design were not clear to the user and we wrote a report with suggestions to improve the interface.

Outcome

It was crucial to have the test planned beforehand. Specially because we wanted to gain the trust of the user and we wanted to demonstrated we were confident on what we were doing. We ran pilot tests which also helped us to correct potential mistakes before the actual test. We switched roles so that we could practice both, however, we did not switch in the middle of the test, so that the user was not confused.

There are some limitations when using PowerPoint as a prototyping tool. One of them is that it takes a lot of time to add the links to the next slides, in order to make a clickable prototype. Another is the lack of wireframe icons, such as buttons and navigation arrows. We therefore had to create every item manually, which made the prototype look less similar to a real interface.

In terms of testing the prototype, it is not always easy to explain the Think Aloud method to the users. In all the tests, we had to probe them, by directly asking what they are thinking, in order to get some data. Otherwise they just keep quiet or they just ask a question when they are stuck. For the next projects, it might be a good idea to show them an example, by conducting the test with my team member. By seeing a practical example, they might understand better what is expected from the test.
